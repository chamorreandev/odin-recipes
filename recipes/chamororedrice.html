Chamorro Red Rice

Chamoro Red Rice, a must-have at any Guam party and in most homes. Itâ€™s just not complete without it! The beautiful reddish orange color is not just pretty to look at, but it also brings plain steamed rice to life! Perfect pairing for island-style BBQ, side-dishes like kelaguen, and other delicious dishes.

ingredients

  • 2 TBS achote seeds
  • 4c very warm water
  • 1 TBL vegetable oil
  • 3c of white rice (I like Nishiki rice)
  • 1/2 yellow onion finely diced
  • 1 TBS Better Than Bouillon Vegetable Base
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

Equipment needed

  • Medium size bowl
  • Mesh strainer
  • Rice cooker

Intructions

  1. In a medium sized bowl, soak achote seeds in warm water with a strainer for about 10 min. After soaking, rub seeds against the strainer to release color. Remove seeds and strainer from water once color is very dark reddish almost brown. Set aside until ready to use.

  2. Rinse uncooked rice and drain, leave in rice cooker until needed.

  3. Heat up vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Saute the diced onions until translucent. Then add in achote water, garlic powder,Better than Bouillon Vegetable Base, and black pepper. Then add to rice and mix until well combined.

  4. Allow to cook in rice cooker (Cook according to the manufacturer instructions), once it is done fluff up the rice gently making sure color is even and rice is completely cooked. Allow to cool before serving.
